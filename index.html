Why I Build Things

My work has always revolved around the same problem: people need clearer information before they can make good decisions.

I started from spaces, property, administration and technical documentation. Over time, I saw the same pattern in different environments: important decisions often depend on information that is scattered, incomplete, difficult to compare or shared too late.

This is what led me to build digital products. Not as abstract design exercises, but as practical ways to organise complexity, guide users and make decision-making feel less confusing.

How I Think

I approach problems by looking first at the information behind them: what people need to know, what is missing, what comes too late and what needs to become easier to compare.
